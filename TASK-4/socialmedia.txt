
Do's for College Students: 

Do showcase your achievements: Use platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to highlight your academic projects, coding challenges, and extracurricular activities.

Do check your privacy settings: Regularly review who can see your posts and personal details across all social platforms to protect your digital footprint.

Do verify before sharing: Always fact-check news or viral messages before forwarding them to avoid spreading misinformation.

Do maintain a respectful tone: Engage politely in online discussions, even when you disagree with someone's opinion.

Do a periodic audit: Clean up your old posts and photos to ensure your public profiles reflect a mature image that future employers or internship coordinators would appreciate.


Don'ts for College Students: 

Don't overshare personal info: Never post sensitive data like your home address, daily schedule, phone number, or banking details publicly.

Don't vent about college/professors: Avoid complaining publicly about your coursework, peers, or faculty, as this leaves a permanent, unprofessional impression.

Don't engage with trolls: If someone is being verbally abusive or trying to pick a fight online, block and report them rather than arguing back.

Don't post inappropriate content: Steer clear of offensive language, discriminatory jokes, or illicit content—these can result in disciplinary action or lost job offers.

Don't plagiarize: Never share someone else's artwork, code, or writing without giving them proper credit.
