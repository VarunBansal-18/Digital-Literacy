Cybercrime Prevention Guidelines

To stay safe from online fraud, students must remember the golden rule of digital payments: a UPI PIN is only ever used to send money, never to receive it. It is equally important to avoid scanning unknown QR codes sent by strangers, as these can trigger unauthorized deductions. Students should protect their personal information by using strong, unique passwords and enabling Two-Factor Authentication on all email, social media, and banking applications. Furthermore, it is crucial to remain cautious of urgent messages or unverified links received via SMS or WhatsApp, as scammers often use fake urgency to induce panic and steal credentials. Keeping device operating systems and banking apps regularly updated will also help patch any security vulnerabilities. Finally, if a student suspects they have been targeted by a scam or notices unauthorized transactions, they should immediately report the incident by calling the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930 or by filing a complaint on the official cybercrime.gov.in portal.
